The Personal Care Products Council is pleased to bring you the
International Buyers’ Guide, 2009. This guide provides the most current
and comprehensive listing of raw materials and their suppliers for the cos-
metic and personal care industry.

Section Descriptions
VOLUME 1
Section 1, Ingredient Suppliers, lists ingredients by Interna-
tional Nomenclature Cosmetic Ingredient (INCI) name. INCI
names are those required to be used on ﬁnished product
labels in the United States, the countries of the European
Union, and many other countries.
Under each INCI name are listed the names of suppliers
of the ingredient. The 2009 edition of the Personal Care
Products Council International Buyers’ Guide contains more
than 17,000 INCI names and their suppliers.
INTRODUCTION
Section 2, Packaging Supplies and Services, contains
listings of ﬁrms that not only supply packaging (bottles,
cartons, etc.), but also those that provide such services as
package design and decoration, inserts for coupons and
directions, and package brochures.
Section 3, Testing Facilities, Equipment & Consultants,
provides a complete listing of ﬁrms that provide such test-
ing services as analytical, environmental, product perfor-
mance, and safety substantiation. This section also lists
suppliers of specialized equipment for all analytical testing
methods and consulting ﬁrms for consumer research, per-
formance testing and claim support, regulatory affairs, and
more.
Section 4, Private Label Manufacturers, lists those ﬁrms
that manufacture cosmetics and personal care products
such as skin care, bath products, and fragrances under a
private label.
Section 5, Suppliers Address Index, contains a listing of all
ﬁrms contained in Sections 1, 2, 3, and 4, and their con-
tact information. There are more than 3,700 suppliers from
nearly 100 countries listed in this edition.
Section 6, Advertisers Index, contains a listing of compa-
nies advertising in the Buyers’ Guide, along with descrip-
tions of their products and services.
VOLUME 2
Section 7, Technical/Trade/INCI Names, contains a listing
of nearly 65,000 common, usual, chemical, and trade
names cross-referenced to more than 17,000 INCI names.
The suppliers of trade names are listed in parentheses im-
mediately following the trade name.
Notice to Users of the International Buyers’ Guide
The International Buyers’ Guide is intended as a reference for locating ingredient suppliers and other service providers to the
cosmetic and personal care industry. This guide should not be used as the source of INCI names for package labels. The
recognized reference for determining the appropriate INCI nomenclature for labeling purposes is the Personal Care Products
Council’s International Cosmetic Ingredient Dictionary and Handbook, Twelfth Edition (2008). This Dictionary provides the
full explanation of differences in the labeling names that may be required for harmonization in the United States, European,
and Japan markets.
In addition, the ingredients appearing in this Buyers’ Guide do not represent an “approved” list of cosmetic ingredients.
The inclusion of any ingredient only means that it has been offered for sale for use in cosmetic products. The assignment of
an INCI name does not imply that the ingredient is “approved,” “certiﬁed,” or “endorsed” by the Personal Care Products Council
or any other organization or governmental body. In addition, the absence of any ingredient name in the Buyers’ Guide does not
imply that the ingredient may not or should not be used in ﬁnished cosmetic products. INCI names do not imply standards or
grades of purity. The suitability of any ingredient, as a component of a ﬁnished cosmetic product or for any other purpose, is the
sole responsibility of the cosmetic product manufacturer, the distributor, or any other users of this Buyers’ Guide.
The names of packaging supplies and services vendors, private label manufacturers, analytical, safety, and testing facilities
and consultants are also provided without review or consideration of their qualiﬁcations as to suitability or standards of
performance. The Personal Care Products Council makes no representation regarding approval, certiﬁcation, or any other
endorsement, and their use is the sole responsibility of the cosmetic product manufacturer, the distributor, or other users of
the Buyers’ Guide.
